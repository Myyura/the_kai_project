# 京都大学 情報学研究科 知能情報学専攻 2025年8月実施 情報学基礎 F1-1

## **Author**
祭音Myyura

## **Description (English)**
### Q.1
Suppose that a sequence $\{x_k \mid k \text{ is a positive integer}\}$ satisfies the recurrence formula,
$$
x_1 = 1, x_2 = 2, x_3 = 1, x_m = a x_{m-1} - a x_{m-2} + b x_{m-3}
$$
where $a$ and $b$ are real numbers, and $m$ is an integer greater than or equal to 4. Let $\boldsymbol{y}_m = (x_m \ x_{m-1} \ x_{m-2})^{\mathrm{T}}$. Answer the following questions, where $n$ is an integer greater than or equal to 4. $\mathrm{I}$ is the identity matrix of order 3, and $A^{\mathrm{T}}$ stands for the transpose of a matrix $A$.

(1) Find a matrix $A$ which satisfies $\boldsymbol{y}_{m+1} = A \boldsymbol{y}_m$.

(2) Find $a$ and $b$ for which $A - \mathrm{I}$ is invertible. For the values $a$ and $b$, determine $(A - \mathrm{I})^{-1}$.

(3) Let $a = -\frac{1}{2}$ and $b = \frac{1}{4}$. Compute the eigenvalues of $A$, and $\lim_{n \to \infty} A^n$.

(4) With the same condition as (3), express $\sum_{k=1}^n x_k$ using $\sum_{m=4}^n \boldsymbol{y}_m$, and compute $\lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{k=1}^n x_k$ using the results of (1) – (3).

### Q.2
In the following, we assume that all elements of matrices are real numbers. In what follows, a matrix with $m$ rows and $n$ columns is called an $m \times n$ matrix. Let $\mathrm{O}_{m \times n}$ be the zero matrix of $m$ rows and $n$ columns, and $\mathrm{I}_n$ be the identity matrix of order $n$. The rank of a matrix $M$ is denoted by $\mathrm{rank}(M)$. Let $A$ be a $k \times m$ matrix, and $B$ be an $m \times n$ matrix. Prove each of the following statements (1) to (5) if it holds, or if it does not hold, give a counterexample of a pair $(A, B)$ and disprove the statement with it.

(1) $\mathrm{rank}(AB) \leq \mathrm{rank}(B)$.

(2) If $k = m$ and $A$ is non-singular, $\mathrm{rank}(AB) = \mathrm{rank}(B)$.

(3) $\mathrm{rank}(A) + \mathrm{rank}(B) - m \leq \mathrm{rank}(AB)$.

(4) If $AB = \mathrm{O}_{k \times n}$, $\mathrm{rank}(A) + \mathrm{rank}(B) \leq m$.

(5) If $n = k$ and $AB = \mathrm{I}_k$, $\mathrm{rank}(A) = k$.

## **Kai**
### Q.1
Let

$$
\boldsymbol{y}_m=
\begin{pmatrix}
x_m\\
x_{m-1}\\
x_{m-2}
\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
x_m=ax_{m-1}-ax_{m-2}+bx_{m-3}.
$$

#### (1)

$$
\boldsymbol{y}_{m+1}=A\boldsymbol{y}_m,
$$

with

$$
\boxed{
A=
\begin{pmatrix}
a&-a&b\\
1&0&0\\
0&1&0
\end{pmatrix}
}.
$$

#### (2)

$$
A-I=
\begin{pmatrix}
a-1&-a&b\\
1&-1&0\\
0&1&-1
\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\det(A-I)=b-1.
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{A-I\text{ is invertible iff }b\ne1}.
$$

For $b\neq 1$,

$$
\boxed{
(A-I)^{-1}
=
\frac{1}{b-1}
\begin{pmatrix}
1&b-a&b\\
1&1-a&b\\
1&1-a&1
\end{pmatrix}
}.
$$

#### (3)

For

$$
a=-\frac12,\qquad b=\frac14,
$$

the characteristic polynomial is

$$
\det(\lambda I-A)
=
\left(\lambda+\frac12\right)
\left(\lambda^2-\frac12\right).
$$

Hence the eigenvalues are

$$
-\frac12,\qquad \frac1{\sqrt2},\qquad -\frac1{\sqrt2}.
$$

All have absolute value less than $1$, so

$$
\boxed{
\lim_{n\to\infty}A^n=O
}.
$$

#### (4)

Since

$$
\boldsymbol{y}_{m+1}-\boldsymbol{y}_m
=
(A-I)\boldsymbol{y}_m,
$$

we have

$$
\sum_{m=4}^{n}\boldsymbol{y}_m
=
(A-I)^{-1}
\left(\boldsymbol{y}_{n+1}-\boldsymbol{y}_4\right).
$$

Here,

$$
x_4=\frac34,
\qquad
\boldsymbol{y}_4=
\begin{pmatrix}
\frac34\\
1\\
2
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Because $\boldsymbol{y}_{n+1}\to\boldsymbol{0}$,

$$
\sum_{m=4}^{n}\boldsymbol{y}_m
\longrightarrow
-(A-I)^{-1}\boldsymbol{y}_4.
$$

Taking the first component,

$$
\lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{k=1}^{n}x_k
=
4+\frac83.
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{
\lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{k=1}^{n}x_k
=
\frac{20}{3}
}.
$$

### Q.2

Let $A$ be a $k\times m$ matrix and $B$ an $m\times n$ matrix.

#### (1)

Since

$$
\operatorname{Im}(AB)
=
A(\operatorname{Im}B),
$$

a linear map cannot increase dimension. Thus,

$$
\boxed{
\operatorname{rank}(AB)\le\operatorname{rank}(B)
}.
$$

### (2)

If $k=m$ and $A$ is invertible, then

$$
B=A^{-1}(AB).
$$

Using part (1) in both directions,

$$
\boxed{
\operatorname{rank}(AB)=\operatorname{rank}(B)
}.
$$

### (3)

Let $U=\operatorname{Im}(B)$. Applying rank-nullity to the restriction $A|_U$,

$$
\operatorname{rank}(B)
=
\operatorname{rank}(AB)
+
\dim(U\cap\ker A).
$$

Since

$$
\dim(U\cap\ker A)
\le
m-\operatorname{rank}(A),
$$

we get

$$
\boxed{
\operatorname{rank}(A)+\operatorname{rank}(B)-m
\le
\operatorname{rank}(AB)
}.
$$

### (4)

If $AB=O$, then

$$
\operatorname{Im}(B)\subseteq\ker(A).
$$

Therefore,

$$
\operatorname{rank}(B)
\le
m-\operatorname{rank}(A),
$$

so

$$
\boxed{
\operatorname{rank}(A)+\operatorname{rank}(B)\le m
}.
$$

### (5)

If $n=k$ and $AB=I_k$, then

$$
k=\operatorname{rank}(AB)\le\operatorname{rank}(A)\le k.
$$

Hence,

$$
\boxed{
\operatorname{rank}(A)=k
}.
$$
